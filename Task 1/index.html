My Favourite Animal

Often referred to as the "sea eagle," the white-tailed eagle (Haliaeetus albicilla) is a large predatory bird that is found throughout temperate Eurasia. Like all eagles, it belongs to the Accipitridae (or accipitrids) family, which also includes harriers, kites, and hawks, among other diurnal raptors. The white-tailed sea-eagle is another name for one of the eleven species in the genus Haliaeetus, commonly known as sea eagles. Depending on the source, it is sometimes referred to as the Eurasian sea eagle, gray sea eagle, or erne.

Cool Facts

  1. White-tailed eagles build massive nests, or eyries, using sticks that can grow to the size of a double bed.
  2. These eagles form strong, lifelong partnerships, pairing up for life and breeding in the same territory each year.
  3. The white-tailed eagle is the largest bird of prey in the UK and Europe, with a wingspan of up to 2.5 meters (about 8 feet).
